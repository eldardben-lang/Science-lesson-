What is this system?The body system of bones, muscles, joints, tendons and ligaments that gives your body shape, support and the ability to move!
🦴
Amazing facts!You have 206 bones and over 600 muscles. Together they make up about 70% of your total body weight!
The Skeleton
Your Amazing Skeleton 🦴
206 bones with 4 vital jobs — tap to explore!
Support 🏗️
The skeleton is your body's framework. It holds you upright and gives your body its shape — without it you'd collapse like jelly!
Protection 🛡️
Bones protect vital organs. The skull shields the brain, the ribcage protects the heart and lungs, and the spine protects the spinal cord.
Movement 🏃
Bones act as levers. When muscles pull on bones, the bones move at joints — like a hinge on a door!
Blood Production 🩸
Red bone marrow inside bones produces red blood cells, white blood cells and platelets — millions every second!
⭐
Did you know?Babies are born with about 270 bones! As you grow, many fuse together. By adulthood you have exactly 206 bones.
Bone Types
4 Types of Bones 🦴
Each shape is perfectly designed for a different job in your body!
Long bones 🦵
Limb levers
Longer than wide — act as levers for movement. Femur (thigh), humerus (upper arm), tibia (shin). Contain yellow marrow for fat storage.
Short bones 🤝
Wrist & ankle
Roughly cube-shaped. Provide stability and some movement. Found in the wrists (carpals) and ankles (tarsals).
Flat bones 🛡️
Protective plates
Thin, flat, often curved. Protect organs and provide muscle attachment. Examples: skull, shoulder blade (scapula), sternum (breastbone), ribs.
Irregular bones 🧩
Unique shapes
Complex shapes that don't fit other categories. Examples: vertebrae (spine), facial bones, hip bones (pelvis).
Inside a bone — what makes it strong?
Compact bone — hard outer layer, gives strength
Spongy bone — light, porous inner layer absorbs force
Bone marrow — soft centre that makes blood cells
Periosteum — tough outer membrane covering the bone
💪
Bone is stronger than concrete!Gram for gram, bone is about 5 times stronger than steel-reinforced concrete. Its hollow, spongy design makes it incredibly strong yet lightweight.

Joints
Joints — Where Bones Meet 🔄
Joints connect bones and allow different types of movement
Ball & socket 🔵
A round bone head sits in a cup-shaped socket. Moves in ALL directions. Found in: ShoulderHip
Hinge joint 🚪
Works like a door hinge — bends and straightens in ONE direction only. Found in: KneeElbowFingers
Pivot joint 🔄
One bone rotates around another. Rotation only. Found in: NeckForearm
Fixed joint 🔒
Bones are fused — NO movement at all. Found in: Skull plates
What is inside a joint?
Cartilage — smooth, rubbery tissue that stops bones grinding together
Ligaments — tough bands that hold bone to bone at the joint
Synovial fluid — slippery fluid that lubricates the joint like oil in an engine
Tendons — connect muscles to bones (ligaments = bone to bone)
🏃
Tendons vs Ligaments!Ligaments link Bone to Bone · Tendons attach muscle to bone. Easy memory trick: Tendons pull on bone to create movement!
Teamwork
Bones & Muscles — The Dream Team! 🤝
Muscles ALWAYS work in pairs — when one contracts, the other relaxes
The bicep / tricep — the classic antagonistic pair
When you BEND your arm:
Bicep contracts (shortens) → pulls forearm UP
Tricep relaxes (lengthens) → lets arm bend
When you STRAIGHTEN your arm:
Tricep contracts → pulls forearm DOWN
Bicep relaxes → lets arm straighten
Step-by-step — what happens when you kick a ball 🦵
1
Your brain sends a nerve signal to the quadriceps muscle (front of thigh)
2
The quadriceps contracts — the tendon pulls on the shin bone (tibia)
3
The knee hinge joint straightens — the lower leg swings forward powerfully
4
The hamstring (back of thigh) relaxes to allow this extension
5
Your foot strikes the ball — force transferred through bones and muscles! ⚽
💡
Antagonistic pairs!Muscles ALWAYS work in pairs — flexors bend joints, extensors straighten them. They take turns contracting and relaxing to give you smooth, controlled movement.
